Results
  • Overview

The plots shown in this dashboard provide an in depth understanding of where facet avalanches are most likely to occur in relation to aspect, as well as how the depth of the facet layer affects the size of the avalanche.

  • Discussion

I found that while southern aspects had the deepest average depth (3.1 ft), they had the least amount of facet released avalanches out of all aspects, while the majority (384) occurred on North Eastern facing slopes. This can be attributed to the difference in sun that each aspect receives. A southern slope receives the most sun out of any, which in the early season can melt early snowfall, reducing the risk of that early snow rotting until the next snowfall. A more northerly aspect does not receive as much sun, allowing a early snow-pack to sit and rot until normal season conditions persist. :::
